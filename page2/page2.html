Linus Torvalds: The driving force behind Linux

Linus Torvalds

Linus was born in Helsinki, the capital and largest city in all of Finland. “He was named after Linus Pauling, the famous physical chemist and Nobel Prize winner.” (2). His family is of the Swedish speaking minority in Finland. A majority of members in his family were journalists including his parents Nils and Anna Torvalds who were both radicals and attended the University of Helsinki during the 60s. His mother was a translator for a Finnish newspaper and also a creator of news graphics, His father also spent a year in Moscow to study and would later on become a radio journalist in the 70s. His parents divorced when he was very young, he lived with his mother and grandparents, He had done a lot of reading at a young age because of the journalists in his family. Although his father tried to push him towards sports and girls and other social activities, ultimately programming and mathematics would be come Linus’s passions.

What is LINUX?

Linux is a UNIX-like operating system, In fact in the README file of the Linux kernel under the “What is Linux” section it states “Linux is a clone of the operating system Unix, written from scratch by Linus Torvalds with assistance from a loosely-knit team of hackers across the Net. It aims towards POSIX and Single UNIX Specification compliance. It has all the features you would expect in a modern fully-fledged Unix, including true multitasking, virtual memory, shared libraries, demand loading, shared copy-on-write executables, proper memory management, and multistack networking including IPv4 and IPv6.” (1)
